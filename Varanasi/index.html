About Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi, is a sacred city in India, renowned for its deep spiritual significance. Situated on the banks of the Ganges River, it is considered one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. For Hindus, Varanasi holds immense religious importance as it is believed to be the abode of Lord Shiva. Pilgrims flock here to perform rituals, seek blessings, and attain moksha (liberation from the cycle of birth and death). The city is a tapestry of ancient temples, ghats, and narrow winding lanes. The ghats, especially Dashashwamedh Ghat and Manikarnika Ghat, are iconic spots where devotees engage in sacred baths and cremation rites, respectively. The daily Ganga Aarti, a devotional ritual involving fire and chanting, is a mesmerizing spectacle that attracts visitors from around the globe. Beyond its spiritual allure, Varanasi is a hub of culture, art, and learning. The city has nurtured many renowned musicians, poets, and scholars. Sarnath, located nearby, is another significant spiritual site where Buddha delivered his first sermon. In Varanasi, the air is thick with devotion, and every corner resonates with an ancient, timeless spirituality that leaves a lasting impression on the soul.

Temples of Varanasi

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Also known as the Golden Temple, it is dedicated to Lord shiva, the presiding deity of the city. Varanasi is Said to be the point at which the first jyotirlinga, the fiery pillar of light by which shiva manifested has supremacy over others gods, broke through the Earth’s crust and flared towards the heavens. More than the Gaths and even the Ganga, the Shivalinga installed in the temple remains the devotional focus of Varanasi.

Tulsi Manas Mandir

Constructed by family of Varanasi, this modern temple is dedicated to Lord Rama. It is situated at the place Where Tulisdas, the great medieval seer, lived and wrote the epic “Shri Ramcharitmanas”, which narrates the life of Lord Rama, the hero of the Ramayana. Verses from Tulidas’s epic are inscribed on the walls.

Kal Bhairav Temple

Devotees believe that Kal Bhairav, the fearsome form of Shiva, protects the city from all harm. Also, God KalBhairav is believed as “Kotwal Of Varanasi” , without his permission no one can stay in Kashi.

Sankat Mochan Mandir

Besides the Assi river stream, on the way of Durga Temple to Banaras Hindu Temple this well known temple of Lord Hanuman is situated. Lord Hanuman is also known as “Sankatmochan” the god who protects from the troubles. This temple is founded by Goswami Tulsidas. This temple is also known as “Monkey” temple, as lot of monkeys are there inside the premises.

Ghats of Varanasi

Dashashwamedh Ghat

The most important ghat where the famous Ganga Aarti takes place every evening.

Manikarnika Ghat

A sacred ghat for cremations, symbolizing the cycle of life and death in Hindu tradition.

Assi Ghat

A peaceful ghat located at the confluence of the Ganges and Assi rivers, popular among pilgrims.

Tulsi Ghat

Named after the great saint Tulsidas, this ghat is known for its connection to Ramayana.
